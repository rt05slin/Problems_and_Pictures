Problem #1 Be there, and Be Square

On a standard 8x8 checkerboard, you can identify 204 unique squares. There are 64 unit squares, 49 2x2 squares, 36 3x3 squares, 25 4x4 squares, 16 5x5 squares, 9 6x6 squares, 4 7x7 squares, and 1 8x8 square. You can find the total number of squares by adding the first eight perfect squares. In general, you can find the number of squares on an NxN checkerboard by adding the first N perfect squares. The sum of the first N perfect squares is N (N + 1) (2N + 1) / 6.

INPUT- a positive integer N less than 1000, the size of a checkerboard

OUTPUT- The number of squares on the NxN checkerboard

EXAMPLE1 − Input: 8 Output: 204

EXAMPLE2 − Input: 10 Output: 385
