Problem #1 The Sum of Perfect Squares

If you look at the simplest examples of sums of perfect squares you may notice a pattern.

12 = 1	
12 + 22 = 5
12 + 22 + 32 = 14
12 + 22 + 32 + 42 = 30
12 + 22 + 32 + 42 + 52 = 55

and

1 = (1) (2) (3) / 6
5 = (2) (3) (5) / 6
14 = (3) (4) (7) / 6
30 = (4) (5) (9) / 6
55 = (5) (6) (11) / 6

so we may conjecture that 12 + 22 + 32 + ... (N − 1)2 + N2 = N (N + 1) (2N + 1) / 6. It can be proved that this is indeed true for all positive integers N.

INPUT − A positive integer N < 1000.

OUTPUT − The sum of the first N perfect squares

EXAMPLE1 − Input: 1 Output: 1

EXAMPLE2 − Input: 5 Output: 55

EXAMPLE3 − Input: 100 Output: 338350
